Standing Setup — applies to every card unless overridden

How to Frame Yourself
Hold the phone vertical (TikTok style). Camera at chest height, arm's length away. Show your face down to the top of your shirt. Look right into the camera lens — not at your own face on the screen.
Lighting
Stand facing a window (best look) OR put a ring light right behind the phone, eye-level. You should see a small bright reflection in your eyes — that's what makes you look sharp on camera.
Background
Pick a clean spot — home office, plain wall, or kitchen counter. Nothing moving behind you. One small brand item is fine (a lamp, a mug, a framed photo).
Sound
Use a clip-on mic if you have one. Otherwise stay within 3 feet of the phone in a quiet room. Don't worry about adding music — CB handles all the editing, sounds, captions, and graphics.
How to Deliver
Skip the "hey guys" — start with the hook line right away. Stick to one idea per video. Say the CTA at the end out loud (CB will add the on-screen text for it).
One Video Per Card
Record each card as ONE single take from start to finish. If you mess up and need to retry, just say "take 2" out loud and start over in the same recording — or send multiple clips and tell CB which one is the keeper. Don't pause and resume mid-recording without saying so. This avoids confusion in editing.
How Often to Shoot
Shoot 5 cards Monday morning. That's a full week of posts. Aim to keep 30 cards ready at all times so you're never scrambling.

Shoot-Day Workflow

Step 01 — Sunday Night
Pick 5 cards
Mix it up — grab at least one gold (serious), one orange (kitchen energy), and one teal (casual/peer). Read the script out loud 3 times. Memorize the first line and the CTA word-for-word. The middle you can say in your own words.
Step 02 — Monday Morning
Set up your spot once
Get your lighting and phone position right before you start. Don't change it between videos. Film the serious ones first (you're sharpest), kitchen ones second, casual/walking ones last.
Step 03 — Monday Morning
2 tries max per video
Your first take is almost always the best. Don't keep redoing it. A real, slightly-imperfect take beats a polished 4th attempt every time.
Step 04 — Send to CB
AirDrop the raw clips
Send raw videos to CB. One video per card unless you took multiple — if you did, just tell CB which take is the keeper. CB handles ALL editing: trending sounds, captions, graphics, hashtags, and scheduling on Instagram, TikTok, and LinkedIn.
